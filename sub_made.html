TOBLERONE CHOCOLATE

HOW IT'S MADE

MANUFACTURING

Toblerone bars are /were produced in Bern. Although the Bernese factory produces a whole lot of chocolate every year, it may not produce the whole world’s supply anymore. The original factory was in Bern’s Länggasse, and all Toblerone was produced there until the 1980s. In the 1980s, Toblerone moved to a bigger factory in Bern-Brünnen, where they continue to produce bars for the world market. Although Toblerone is no longer a Swiss company—it was bought by Kraft in 1990, which then became Mondelez—most production stays within Switzerland.

HOW IT’S MADE

How the Toblerone chocolate bar is made:

You need 3 ingredients: cocoa beans, sugar, milk powder. Cocoa beans have to be roasted in small pan. This turns the cocoa beans dark brown. The beans appealed crushed and then finally ground. Everything looks almost like liquid chocolate. ( This is now pure cocoa mass. ) Now we mix other ingredients with a cocoa mass, we add the sugar and milk powder. The long stirring time makes the chocolate soft and creamy. Now the unique Toblerone ingredient has to be added - the nougat pieces. It takes the honey and boils it until small bubbles. While the honey is heating we mix the icing sugar, a little water and balls that up as well. We mix everything together and balls all of it up together once more. Once the mixture is cooled a little, we whip the separated white egg into stiff peaks and add honey syrup to it. Now the almonds have to be added and mixed in. The mixture has to cool before can be chopped up. We can mix in nougat pieces with the chocolate and pour it all into the Toblerone mold. Pour it in give it a shake so that it flows into the mold evenly. Let it cool until the chocolate is firm. Turn the mold upside down, shake it a bit and there is our homemade Toblerone chocolate bar.
